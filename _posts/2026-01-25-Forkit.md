## Decide on where to eat in 10 minutes or less
I have been working on a [project on lovable](https://stickaforkinit.lovable.app). 
Leveraging ChatGPT, I came up with the [PRD](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1bTVLho2cbG1ytUWxzHLJDMLWRCIbTMI88ev2bftljrA/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.dafhnnultocn)


Deciding where to eat as a group should be simple. In reality, it’s a slow, frustrating, and surprisingly high-friction process.

The pattern is familiar:
* Someone asks, “What do you want to eat?” or "Where do you want to eat?"
* Responses range from “I don’t care” to wildly different preferences.
* Ideas live across text threads, Notes apps, screenshots, or someone’s mental list.
* Constraints like price, cuisine, location, and availability surface late, if at all.
* The group either defaults to the same safe choice or spends far too long debating.

What should take minutes often takes 30+ minutes, multiple back-and-forths, and still ends in mild dissatisfaction. This product allows for users to submit their ideas, and everybody with that link can vote, similar to ranked choice voting.
